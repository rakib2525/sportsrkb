## Common batting points

Fantasy cricket scoring often starts with batting. Runs are usually the easiest action to understand. A generic system may give one point per run. Boundaries may add small bonuses because fours and sixes show scoring impact.

Strike rate can also matter in some systems, especially in limited-overs cricket. A fast innings may receive a bonus, while a very slow innings may receive a penalty. Because these rules vary, a generic calculator should clearly show the point values it uses.

## Common bowling points

Wickets are usually valuable because they change the match quickly. A generic system may give a larger score for each wicket. Maiden overs can also earn points because they show complete run control for an over.

Economy rate may be used as a bonus or penalty in some scoring systems. A bowler who concedes very few runs can be rewarded, while a very expensive spell may lose points. Again, exact thresholds vary.

## Fielding points

Catches, run outs, and stumpings are common fielding actions. They can matter a lot because a player who does little with bat or ball may still contribute in the field.

A generic system may give points for each catch and a larger value for run outs or stumpings. Some platforms split run-out points between players involved, but a generic guide should avoid pretending one rule applies everywhere.

## Bonus points

Bonuses can include half-centuries, centuries, multiple wickets, high strike rate, low economy, or player-of-the-match style awards. Not every system uses these. Some systems also include negative points for ducks, expensive bowling, or fielding mistakes.

The safest approach is to use a transparent generic system and explain that real platform rules may differ.

## Why generic scoring is useful

A generic fantasy calculator helps users estimate contribution without copying a branded platform's rules. It is useful for learning how batting, bowling, and fielding actions combine into one score.

It should not claim to match any specific app or contest. It should not use player photos, team logos, live feeds, or official fantasy branding.

## Practical reminder

Before entering a contest anywhere, read that contest's actual scoring rules. Use generic tools for education and planning, not as a guarantee of official fantasy points.
