## Economy by phase

Powerplay overs often have fielding restrictions, so boundaries can be easier to find. Middle overs may reward bowlers who build pressure. Death overs can be expensive because batters are actively searching for boundaries.

## What counts as good

Instead of using one fixed number, compare the bowler's economy to the match scoring rate and the role they were asked to perform. A death bowler can bowl well even with a higher economy than a middle-overs bowler.

## Combine it with other numbers

Economy rate is strongest when read with dot ball percentage, wickets, boundary control, and extras. A low economy with no wickets can still be valuable if it creates pressure for the next bowler.

## Phase-based interpretation

Powerplay economy can be difficult to judge because fielding restrictions create scoring chances. Middle-over economy often shows control because batters are trying to rotate strike and set up the finish. Death-over economy is usually higher because batters attack almost every ball.

That is why a single "good" number can mislead. A bowler conceding 8 per over at the death may have done a useful job in a high-scoring match, while the same number in the middle overs may be expensive.

## Use with spell analysis

The bowler's spell analyzer combines economy, dots, boundaries, extras, and wickets. That fuller view is more useful than judging a spell by economy alone.
